Exploring the Impact of Smoking Worldwide

Smoking remains a significant global health concern. It contributes to various diseases, economic burdens, and societal impacts.

Our visualizations explore the prevalence of smoking and its effects on public health. Understanding these trends is critical for effective intervention and policy-making.

Smoking Prevalence by Country

The choropleth map highlights smoking rates globally. Differences in rates reflect cultural, economic, and public health factors.

Countries with higher saturation levels indicate higher smoking rates.

* Hover to view smoking rate per country. Use the slider to filter.

Top 10 Countries by Cigarette Consumption

This bar chart ranks countries based on cigarette consumption. Cultural attitudes, economics, and regulations can impact these rankings.

* Hover to view specific consumption values per country.

Smoking Rate Trends Over Time

This line chart visualizes smoking rate changes over time. The data tracks trends across different countries to evaluate how smoking behavior evolves.

* Select a country and adjust the time range using the timeline.

Smoking Rates: Male vs. Female

This butterfly chart compares smoking rates between males and females. Gender-specific behaviors and cultural factors often drive differences.

* Hover over bars to view gender-specific rates.

Smoking Rates vs. Healthcare Spending

This bubble plot reveals the relationship between smoking rates and healthcare spending. Larger bubbles indicate larger populations, while color represents continent.

* Pan and zoom to explore regions. Larger bubbles represent bigger populations.
